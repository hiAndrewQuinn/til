## Tagging Notes

- `todays-lucky-10000` — Apply when the post explains something that many people don't know but probably should. Named after the xkcd comic.
- `against-entropy` — Apply when the post is about fighting back against disorder, complexity creep, or bit rot.
- `bad-automation` — Apply when the post is about automation gone wrong or the perils of automating the wrong things.
- `simple-suboptimal-solutions` — Apply when the post champions a solution that is good enough and easy, even if not optimal.
- `worse-is-better` — Apply when the post is about the Unix/Richard Gabriel tradition of valuing simplicity of implementation over correctness or completeness.
- `the-medium-is-the-message` — Apply when the post is about how the choice of tool or format shapes the content itself (McLuhan-flavored).
- `econ-flavored` — Apply when the post uses economic reasoning or concepts without being strictly about economics.
- `psych-flavored` — Apply when the post uses psychological concepts or cognitive science without being strictly about psychology.
- `law-of-diminishing-returns` — Apply when the post is about diminishing returns in any domain.
- `complementary-goods` — Apply when the post discusses how two things become more valuable together.
- `principal-agent-problems` — Apply when the post is about misaligned incentives between parties.
